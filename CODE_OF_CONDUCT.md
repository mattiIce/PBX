# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, and inclusive community for everyone contributing to and participating in the PBX System project. We value diversity of experience, skill levels, and backgrounds, and strive to create an environment where all contributors feel respected and empowered to collaborate effectively.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

We encourage behaviors that promote a healthy and productive community:

- **Respectful Communication**: Use considerate language and be mindful of different perspectives
- **Constructive Feedback**: Provide helpful suggestions and accept criticism gracefully
- **Collaborative Spirit**: Work together to solve problems and improve the project
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Help others learn and grow through patient guidance
- **Professional Conduct**: Maintain professionalism in all project interactions
- **Inclusive Language**: Use welcoming words that make everyone feel valued
- **Active Listening**: Consider others' viewpoints before responding
- **Recognition**: Acknowledge the contributions and efforts of others

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in our community:

- **Harassment**: Any form of intimidation, bullying, or unwelcome attention
- **Trolling**: Deliberately inflammatory or disruptive comments
- **Personal Attacks**: Insults or derogatory remarks about individuals
- **Discrimination**: Prejudicial treatment based on personal characteristics
- **Privacy Violations**: Sharing others' private information without consent
- **Disruptive Conduct**: Sustained disruption of discussions or collaboration
- **Inappropriate Content**: Sharing offensive, explicit, or unsuitable material
- **Dishonesty**: Misrepresenting facts, plagiarism, or claiming others' work

## Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. They have the authority to take appropriate action in response to unacceptable behavior, including:

- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, issues, and contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently banning contributors who exhibit inappropriate, threatening, or harmful behavior
- Providing guidance and warnings when minor violations occur

Maintainers commit to applying these standards fairly and consistently across all community interactions.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews)
- Project communication channels (chat, email, forums)
- Project events (meetings, conferences, workshops)
- Public representation of the project (social media, presentations, media)

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

### Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it promptly:

- **Maintainer Contact**: Reach out directly to project maintainers through GitHub direct messages or email
- **GitHub Issues**: For non-sensitive reports, you may open a public issue in the repository
- **Email**: Contact the project team through official channels for confidential matters

When reporting, please include:
- Description of the incident
- Names of individuals involved (if comfortable sharing)
- Context and any supporting information
- Your preferred resolution

All reports will be reviewed confidentially and addressed promptly.

### Enforcement Process

1. **Review**: Maintainers will investigate all reports thoroughly and impartially
2. **Response**: We will communicate with the reporter about the findings and actions taken
3. **Action**: Appropriate measures will be taken based on the severity:
   - **Warning**: For minor or first-time violations
   - **Temporary Ban**: For repeated or moderate violations
   - **Permanent Ban**: For severe or persistent violations
4. **Appeals**: Individuals may appeal decisions through the same reporting channels
